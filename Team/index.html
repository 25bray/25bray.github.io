Epilepsy

What is Epilepsy?

  • Epilepsy is a brain disorder
  • It causes you to have seizures
  • Most of the time these seizures are caused by flashing lights and other sudden movements.

    • What is a Seizure?

  • During a seizure, there are bursts of electrical activity happening in your brain.
  • But seizures are different for everyone that actually has Epilepsy

    • There are a lot of kinds of seizures such as:

  • Tonic-Clonic Seizure
  • Tonic Seizure
  • Clonic Seizure
  • Absence Seizure

    • What is a EEG?

  • Electroencephalography - (EEG) is a test that measures the electrical activity in your brain. It's done by placing small sensors on your scalp, which detect the signals coming from your brain cells. These signals are then recorded as wavy lines on a computer. Doctors use EEG to check for problems like seizures or sleep disorders, and it helps them understand how your brain is working. It's painless and doesn't require any needles or injections.

    • What are the Seizure First Aid Procedures:

  • Time the seizure
  • Check for medical ID
  • Keep the person safe during seizure.
  • Move or guide them away from harm
  • Turn the person onto their SIDE if they are not awake or aware.
  • Keep airway clear
  • Loosen any tight clothes around neck
  • Place something small and soft under their head to prevent head injuries
  • Stay with the person until they are awake and alert after the seizure.

    • If or at what point should I call 911?

  • Seizure lasts longer that 5 minutes
  • They don't return to their usual state
  • They are injured
  • Prenant
  • Sick
  • Repeated seizures
  • First time seizure
  • Difficulty breathing
  • Seizure occurs in water

    • Do Not:

  • Restrain them
  • Put any objects in their mouth

    • Rescue Medications can be given if prescribed by a health care professional
